\section{Conclusion}

In this work, we investigated the adversarial and out-of-distribution robustness of neural thicket ensembles. We demonstrated that standard \randopt ensembles, while effective for clean performance, can severely degrade \ood accuracy due to over-correlated experts. To address this, we proposed \methodname, which pairs opposing weight perturbations to enforce weight-space diversity. Our experiments on the \rgsm benchmark show that \methodname significantly improves \ood robustness (from 5.0\% to 15.0\%) and provides measurable gains against adversarial noise. These results emphasize that explicit diversity is a necessary condition for building resilient weight-space ensembles. Future work will explore applying Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization to further increase expert diversity and scaling these methods to larger foundation models.
